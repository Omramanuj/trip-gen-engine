Career Passport · Design System v1

Departures.

A warm, editorial system for a chat-first career platform. Built on the soft geometry of a travel app — cream canvas, teal surfaces, coral action, sky-blue information — reframed around one idea: a candidate's assessment isn't a test, it's a journey they depart on.

The metaphor
Assessment→ a Trip
Chat home→ the Terminal
Past chats→ Past Journeys
Proof / artifacts→ Stamps in the passport
Job prep→ the Itinerary
Color roles
Cream — the canvas. Calm, paper-like, always the ground.
Teal — surfaces of focus & verified proof.
Coral — the one action that matters on a screen.
Sky — information, status, the candidate's own data.

One coral CTA per screen. If everything shouts, nothing does.

Principles

Voice & usage

The metaphor only works if it's disciplined. A few rules keep "Departures" from drifting into theme-park territory.

Do
  • Keep one coral action per screen — the departure.
  • Use teal for proof and focus, sky for the candidate's own data and status.
  • Let cream breathe — generous negative space is the luxury cue.
  • Speak warmly and concretely: "Resume your trip," "3 of 8 stamps."
  • Round everything; soft, warm-tinted shadows only.
Don't
  • Don't overload the travel metaphor in copy — it's a frame, not a costume. No "fasten your seatbelt."
  • Don't put coral on data or status — that's sky's job.
  • Don't use grey system shadows or pure-black text.
  • Don't stack multiple teal panels — one focus surface at a time.
  • Don't let stat tiles imply a score the system can't defend — they show evidence, not verdicts.

Implementation notes

Every value here is a CSS custom property (see :root in this file's source) so it ports cleanly into Tailwind theme tokens or a tokens.json for your Next.js app. Fonts are Google-hosted (Fraunces, Hanken Grotesk, Geist Mono). The Trip card's 3D motif is inline SVG — swap it for your rendered passport/compass asset when you have one, keeping the same coral-needle-on-sky-disc composition so the silhouette stays recognisable.
